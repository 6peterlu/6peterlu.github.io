Involves: Project management, Networking, Communication

Over the half-year grind that is organizing Stanford's premier hackathon, I discovered that computer science is more than code on a screen. > Biocomputation Researcher at Koong Lab

Involves: Python, Cytoscape, Torch, Lab techniques, Clinical shadowing

I started at the Koong Lab testing experimental drugs against XBP1s in breast cancer cells, but my role evolved over time to leveraging my scripting ability to improve the efficiency of the lab in other ways. I automated several segments of our data processing pipeline as part of a large Stanford Hospital project to predict prognoses of patients based on natural language processing of their clinical notes. Computer science has tremendous potential to improve healthcare for all, and I plan to be on the forefront of those efforts.

projects

> Deep Convolutional Neural Networks for Lung Cancer Detection

Involves: Tensorflow, Numpy, Kaggle Data Science Bowl 2017 dataset, Google Cloud

link to paper

Numpy for data preprocessing, Tensorflow for neural network framework. Created an attention network to first identify regions of interest in CT scans, and fed these regions of interest into a classifier network sharing a similar architecture to GoogLeNet, but adapted for 3D data. Final AUC was much improved over using a 3D classifier alone.

> Threadpool

Wrote a thread manager, and used it to pull news articles off of the internet, as well as support basic MapReduce. Tackled issues with concurrency and multithreading using mutexes, semaphores, and condition variable any.
